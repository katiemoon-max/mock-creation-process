# House Style Formatting Rules

These rules apply to all SME content. They are derived from the SME House Style Guide (V1) and the Revision Notes Style Guide.

## Ampersands & Conjunctions

- Use **&** in H1 and H2 headings
- Use **"and"** in body text, H3, and H4 headings
- Exception: ampersands in proper names or abbreviations (e.g. "AT&T") are always kept

## Punctuation

- **No Oxford comma** — do not use a serial comma before "and" in lists
- **No terminal punctuation** on bullet points (no full stops, colons, or semicolons at end of bullets)
- Single space after full stops
- UK date format: 1 January 2026 (not January 1, 2026)
- Use en dashes (–) for ranges, not hyphens

## Capitalisation

- **H1**: Title Case (capitalise major words)
- **H2, H3, H4**: Sentence case (capitalise first word and proper nouns only)
- H2 headings use ampersands (&), H3 and H4 use "and"
- Subjects are lowercase unless part of an official specification title
- Exam board names always capitalised: AQA, OCR, Edexcel, CIE, IB, AP

## Heading Hierarchy

- Never skip heading levels (H1 > H2 > H3 > H4, no gaps)
- H1 is the page title only (one per page)
- H4 headings used for image labels
- Heading hierarchy contributes to CQI "Structured" criterion

## Text Formatting

- **Bold** for emphasis and key terms
- *Italics* for examples, species names, titles of texts, and mathematical variables in prose
- **Never** use underlining
- **Never** use ALL CAPS for emphasis
- No emoji in content

## Numbers

- Numbers **1-10** written in words (one, two, three...)
- Numbers **11+** written in digits (11, 12, 13...)
- Always leave a space between a number and its unit (e.g. "5 m" not "5m")
- Use the equation editor consistently for mathematical expressions

## Spelling & Language

- **UK English** for all boards except AP
- **American English** for AP courses only
- Follow the exam board's own spelling of key terms (check the specification)
- Fallback reference: The Guardian style guide

## Bullet Points

- Maximum 3-4 lines per bullet point
- Break long sentences into multiple bullets
- Keep bullets concise — favour fragments over full sentences
- Use consistent grammatical structure within a list (parallel construction)

## Images & Diagrams

- Diagrams must use **black, grey, and white only** — no colour
- Alt text is **required** on all images
- Use H4 headings for image labels/captions
- Images must support learning, not decoration
- Only use CC-licensed photos
- Image placeholders in drafts: `[IMAGE: description of required diagram]`

## Callout Boxes (Cobalt CMS)

- **Worked Examples** (Green): Question + Answer; marks right-aligned; "Answer:" in bold black
- **Examiner Tips & Tricks**: Placed where most relevant (not grouped); 2nd person voice; common errors, tips, misconceptions
- **Mark Scheme & Guidance**: Marking points, accepted alternatives, command term guidance, alternate methods

## Links & References

- Use descriptive link text (not "click here")
- External links open in a new window
- Only link to reputable sources
- Use "SME" or "Save My Exams" when referencing the company
- Avoid real brand names in examples — invent alternatives
- Harvard-style citations for academic references

## Popovers

- Used for key term definitions
- First occurrence of a term only
- Sentence case for popover text
- Bold blue text indicates a popover link

## Accessibility

- Target WCAG AAA standards
- Well-spaced, uncluttered layout
- Bullet points to highlight key information
- Alt text on all images
- Subtitles on video content
